Message of Secretary FE&WD

Mr. Muhammad Abid Majeed

It’s a moment of pride to express that in pursuance to the good governance policy of the government, the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Wildlife Department has developed Management Information System (MIS) with shared database for compilation of data into a wholesome digitized program. It is a shift from conventional record keeping to advanced technological development which will contribute to efficient, proactive and convenient working processes through improved automation. ...In fact, it’s the need of the current development scenario to adopt progressive approaches towards proficient use of technological means. The need for use of such approaches intensified with the passage of time and multiple technology based programs have been initiated in the government departments/ organizations. As an outcome, efficiency in task management will be achieved for evolving proactive strategies and long term planning. It will enhance ability of the department to respond to issues timely. The newly developed website, MIS with shared database and mobile app will enable the department to access the diversified information just on a click. The initiative is also intended to cater for public service delivery such as application for issuance of shooting and possession licences, etc.

Muhammad Hussain

About

Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Wildlife Department which was notified as an attached Department in August, 1994, started as wing of Forest Department in 1975. The Wildlife Organization in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Province emerged in 1974 as an ex-cadre wing of the Forest Department under the administrative control of Chief Conservator of Forests. This wing functioned for 4 years as a non significant and low priority organization and was ultimately abolished in 1978 under an economy drive. Conservation of wildlife again became a priority and the wildlife wing was re-established in 1981. Conservation awareness and priority reached a high mark in 1995 when the wildlife wing was given the status of an "Attached Department" within Forest, Fisheries and Wildlife Department and Conservator Wildlife was declared as Head of the Department.
